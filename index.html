History

Established in 1988, Trapholt is a Danish museum in Kolding, known for its focus on Danish arts and crafts. Its name, a combination of "Tr√¶" (wood) and "holt" (grove), reflects its emphasis on Danish arts and crafts. The iconic building, designed by architect Hans Hollein, overlooks Kolding Fjord. With collections spanning modern art and design, the museum hosts diverse exhibitions featuring Danish and international artists. Committed to education, Trapholt engages visitors of all ages. Surrounded by scenic gardens, it serves as a cultural hub, contributing to Denmark's artistic landscape while continuously evolving to preserve and promote Danish design heritage.
